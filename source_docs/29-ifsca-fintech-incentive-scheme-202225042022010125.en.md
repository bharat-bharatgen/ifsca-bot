# IFSCA FinTech Incentive Scheme 2022

**Source:** The Gazette of India: Extraordinary [Part III—Sec.4]
**Notification No:** IFSCA/2021-22/GN/022
**Date:** Gandhinagar, 2nd February, 2022
**Authority:** International Financial Services Centres Authority
**Chairperson:** Injeti Srinivas

---

## 1. Objective

(1) The principal objective of the scheme is to promote the establishment of a world class FinTech Hub, comparable with those located in advanced International Financial Centers (IFCs) across jurisdictions, at International Financial Services Centre (IFSC) in India by providing financial support to FinTech activities in the form of specific grant(s) as specified in the scheme, based on their eligibility and fulfilment of terms and conditions as may be specified.

(2) This scheme shall be open to:
- a) Domestic FinTechs seeking access to overseas markets
- b) Domestic FinTechs seeking listing on IFSCA recognised stock exchanges
- c) Foreign FinTechs seeking market access to IFSCs in India and work within the Authority's regulatory framework
- d) Foreign FinTechs seeking access to domestic market under Inter-Operable Regulatory Sandbox (IORS) framework
- e) Domestic FinTechs extending business to the IFSCs either by way of authorisation or registration or through the regulatory sandbox

---

## 2. Short Title and Commencement

(1) The scheme may be called the **International Financial Services Centres Authority (FinTech Incentive) Scheme, 2022**.

(2) This scheme shall come into force on the date of its publication in the official gazette and shall be operational for an initial period of **three years** from the date of commencement.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Act** | International Financial Services Centres Authority Act, 2019 (50 of 2019) |
| **Accelerator/Cohort** | Business programmes run by companies or incubators that supports early-stage, growth-driven FinTech companies through education, mentorship, etc. |
| **Applicant** | An eligible FinTech Entity who makes an application to the Authority for receiving grant under this Scheme |
| **Domestic FinTech Entity** | A resident individual or an entity incorporated or established in India including in the IFSCs, regulated by the Authority under the appropriate framework or regulations |
| **Evaluation Committee** | Committee set up by the Authority for evaluation and processing of applications made under this Scheme |
| **Financial Institution** | Same meaning as under clause (c) of sub-section (1) of Section 3 of the Act |
| **FinTech** | A technologically enabled innovative solution aiding or assisting Financial Institutions in delivery of Financial Products or Financial Services |
| **FinTech Entity (FE)** | A Domestic FinTech Entity or a Foreign FinTech Entity |
| **Foreign FinTech Entity** | A non-resident entity from outside India which is regulated by the Authority to undertake FinTech related activities in the IFSC |
| **Incentive** | Monetary grant given to the FinTech Entities under the terms and conditions of this Scheme |
| **Innovation Sandbox** | Testing environment where FinTech Entities can test their ideas and solutions in isolation from the live market, based on data made available by Financial Institutions operating in IFSC |
| **Internal Committee** | Committee constituted by the Authority for monitoring the end use of grants given under this Scheme |
| **IFSC** | International Financial Services Centre - same meaning as under clause (g) of sub-section (1) of Section 3 of the Act |
| **Inter-Operable Regulatory Sandbox (IORS)** | IORS testing of innovative hybrid financial products/services falling within the regulatory ambit of more than one domestic financial sector regulator |
| **Minimum Viable Product (MVP)** | A version of a product with minimum features for providing feedback for future product development by the early users |
| **Proof-of-Concept (PoC)** | A demonstration of a concept, principle or idea about its feasibility and potential |
| **Prototype** | An early sample, model, or release of a product built to test a concept or process |
| **Regulatory Sandbox** | A live environment with a limited set of real customers for a limited timeframe wherein entities shall be granted certain exemptions/relaxations from applicability of certain regulatory provisions for experimenting FinTech ideas and solutions |

## 4. Eligibility

### (1) Eligible Applicants

#### From India:
- a) Entity registered with DPIIT (Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade) as a start-up entity relating to FinTech
- b) Entity incorporated as a company under the Companies Act 2013, or as a Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) under the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008 or a 'Branch' of an Indian company or LLP in IFSC
- c) An Individual who is a resident citizen
- d) An entity working directly or indirectly in the ecosystem regulated by RBI, SEBI, IRDAI or PFRDA

#### From Outside India:
- A Non-resident Individual or an entity from **FATF compliant countries/jurisdictions**
- **Proviso:** Where the applicant is a body corporate incorporated outside India, the shareholding of non-resident Individuals in the applicant shall, at all times, be more than **51%**

### (2) Technology Requirement
The Applicant undertakes to use technology in its core product or service, business model, distribution model or methodology to solve the problem being targeted.

### (3) No Double Funding
An entity who has received any grant earlier from the Central or State Government schemes for the same project scope and activities shall not be eligible to apply under this Scheme.
- **Exception:** Prize money from competitions and grand-challenges, founder monthly allowance, etc. shall not be considered as grant received earlier.

### (4) Separate Proposals
Applicant shall be required to submit separate proposals if there is more than one project or innovative components - each shall be treated as separate projects and evaluated and incentivised separately.

---

## 5. Scope of the Incentive Scheme

The Grants shall be available to eligible FEs who:
1. Are part of the Authority's Regulatory or Innovative Sandbox
2. Participate in the Authority's supported Accelerators or Cohorts or Special Programs
3. Are referred to by entity(ies) including regulatory or supervisory bodies having MoU or collaboration or special arrangement with the Authority

---

## 6. Types of Incentives

| Grant Type | Maximum Amount | Purpose |
|------------|----------------|---------|
| **FinTech Start-up Grant** | Rs. 15 lacs (₹1.5 Million) | Developing a product or service and related 'go-to-market' initiatives for a start-up with a novel FinTech idea. Focus is on converting the idea into an MVP. Covers product development, manpower costs, IT costs, etc. |
| **Proof of Concept (PoC) Grant** | Rs. 50 lacs (₹5.0 Million) | Conducting a PoC by an early or mature FE in domestic market or overseas. Covers manpower costs, IT costs, marketing, prototyping costs, etc. |
| **Sandbox Grant** | Rs. 30 lacs (₹3.0 Million) | Experimenting with innovative products or services in a sandbox. Covers software development, prototyping, manpower, consulting, tech-related, IT, admin costs, etc. |
| **Green FinTech Grant** | Rs. 75 lacs (₹7.5 Million) | Developing solutions facilitating sustainable finance and sustainability linked finance, including ESG investments |
| **Accelerator Grant** | Rs. 10 lacs (₹1.0 Million) | Supporting accelerators at IFSC for capacity building, building capabilities around mentors, bringing investors, bringing more projects or PoC, tie-ups, etc. |
| **Listing Support Grant** | Rs. 15 lacs (₹1.5 Million) | Supporting Domestic FEs aspiring to list on stock exchanges recognised by the Authority. Covers road shows, international travel and listing requirements, etc. |

## 7. Execution of the Scheme

### (1) Process

1. **Application:** Applicant shall make the application in the form and manner, containing such particulars and accompanied by such documents and fees, as may be specified by the Authority
2. **Scrutiny:** Authority shall scrutinize the application and conduct due diligence with respect to eligibility, compliance with regulatory requirements including KYC-AML guidelines, disclosure norms, corporate governance norms
3. **Evaluation:** Application shall be evaluated by an Evaluation Committee which shall submit its recommendation along with comments and observations
4. **Sanction:** Authority may issue a sanction letter containing specific terms & conditions of the grant
5. **Monitoring:** Internal Committee shall monitor pre and post disbursement conditions, compliance, end use of grant, progress, milestone achievements
6. **Disbursement:** Made subject to satisfactory compliance of terms and conditions and achieving requisite milestones

### (2) Conditions for Sanction of Grant

1. FE shall need proper authorization and delegation of power in favour of its representative
2. FE or its project team shall operate from IFSC during the sandbox or accelerator period
3. On successful completion of Sandbox/accelerator/cohort/special program, FE shall incorporate an entity at IFSC and seek registration/license/authorisation under the Act
4. FE shall undertake **not to implement a successful solution using the grant in any other jurisdiction for a period of 3 years**
5. If FE fails to meet any conditions, FE shall return the grant along with **simple interest at the rate of 8% per annum**

### (3) Disbursement of Grants

1. Disbursement shall be linked to milestones set for the FE, decided in consultation with Internal Committee
2. Disbursement shall be done on **'reimbursement' basis** after submission of necessary documents, invoices, technical reports
3. Authority shall normally release disbursement within **30 working days** of submission of all documents
4. **Advance under the scheme may be considered only under exceptional circumstances**
