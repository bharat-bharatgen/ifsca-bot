# I. General Introductory Questions

### 1. What is GIFT-CITY?

Gujarat International Finance Tec-City (GIFT City) is an emerging global financial and IT services hub to facilitate the delivery of a wide range of financial services globally.

GIFT City is a Smart City and a planned commercial business hub located in Gandhinagar, Gujarat. It is a greenfield project providing modern amenities for commercial, residential and social purposes.

**Two parts to GIFT City:**
- I. Domestic Tariff Area (DTA)
- II. Multi-service Special Economic Zone (GIFT SEZ)

GIFT SEZ is India's maiden International Financial Services Centre (IFSC) set up under section 18 of the Special Economic Zone Act, 2005 ("SEZ Act 2005")

### 2. What is the difference between IFSC, GIFT-IFSC and IFSCA?

**International Financial Services Centre (IFSC):**
- Section 18 of the SEZ Act, 2005 provides for setting up of an IFSC in a Special Economic Zone (SEZ) post approval of the Central Government
- Central Government approved for setting up country's maiden IFSC at the GIFT City, Gandhinagar
- An IFSC enables entities to cater to customers outside the jurisdiction of the domestic economy
- Such centres deal with flows of finance, financial products and financial services across borders in "Specified foreign currencies"
- The IFSC seeks to bring back those financial services transactions currently carried on outside India by overseas financial institutions and overseas branches/subsidiaries of Indian financial institutions to the Indian shores

**Gujarat International Finance Tec International Financial Services Centre (GIFT-IFSC):**
- India's first IFSC located in GIFT City, Gandhinagar, Gujarat, India
- Provides world-class infrastructure and services for financial institutions operating in banking, insurance, capital markets, funds management and other financial products, services or institutions

**International Financial Services Centres Authority (IFSCA):**
- Statutory authority established by Government of India on **April 27, 2020**, under the IFSCA Act, 2019
- Mandate to regulate financial products, services, or institutions in IFSC
- Unified regulator for Banking, Capital Market, Insurance, Pension funds and other financial products, services, or institutions in IFSC
- Section 13 of the IFSCA Act empowers IFSCA to exercise all powers exercisable by RBI, SEBI, IRDAI and PFRDA under their respective Acts

**Main Objective:** Develop a strong global connect and focus on the needs of the Indian economy as well as to serve as an international financial platform for the entire region and the global economy.

# II. FinTech Entity Framework (Authorization and Limited Use Authorisation)

### 1. What is the recognition given by IFSCA to Entity engaged in providing Technology oriented solutions?

IFSCA Circular dated **April 27, 2022**, on "Framework for FinTech Entity in the IFSCs" ("FE Framework") empowers IFSCA to grant Authorization or Limited Use Authorization to eligible domestic and foreign entities as a "FinTech Entity" ('FE") for carrying out fintech or techfin activities enumerated in Annexure-I of the FE Framework.

Entities can apply to IFSCA to obtain Authorization, without entering into the Sandbox via **SWIT Portal**.

### 2. What is the difference between FinTech and TechFin under the FinTech Entity Framework of IFSCA?

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **FinTechs** | Entities providing financial technology solutions that result in new business models, applications, process, or products in financial services regulated by IFSCA |
| **TechFins** | Entities that provide an advanced/emerging technology solution(s) which aid and assists activities in relation to financial products, financial services, and financial institutions |

### 3. Which entities are eligible to apply under the FE Framework?

**1. If Applicant is from India:**
- I. An entity registered with DPIIT as a start-up related to FinTech; or
- II. A company or LLP incorporated in India; or
- III. Branch of an Indian Company or LLP in IFSC; or
- IV. An entity working directly or indirectly in the ecosystem regulated by RBI/SEBI/IRDAI/PFRDA

**2. If Applicant is from Outside India:**
- An entity from any jurisdictions **except** those identified in the public statement of FATF as "High-Risk Jurisdictions subject to a Call for Action" (i.e. black list)

**b) The entity shall undertake one of the activities under the permissible areas/activities of FE Framework.**

### 6. For seeking authorization, is profits (Gross/Net) mandatory criteria?

For an Entity seeking Authorization, a **revenue earning track record in at least one of the last three financial years is mandatory**.

### 7. When is a Security Assessment Report required?

The Entity shall at all times ensure proper cyber security systems to protect against cyber threats, data leakage, payment security, etc.

**For Authorization:** Entity shall submit Security Assessment Report if available (Security Audit Certificate/VAPT Certificate):
- From **Cert-In empanelled Auditor** – if Indian Entity
- From an **equivalent nodal Agency** – if Entity is from outside India

IFSCA may mandate the Security Assessment Report for certain entities applying for Limited Use Authorization (Sandbox).

# IV. Accelerator

### 1. What is an Accelerator?

An accelerator is an entity whose focus/core area is to provide support to start-ups/growth-driven entities by conducting cohorts to enable them to scale fast. The support can be in the form of resources (people and money), mentors, connections, knowledge, etc.

### 2. What is the recognition given by IFSCA to Accelerators?

An accelerator seeking Authorization or Limited Use Authorization under the FE Framework shall conduct cohorts as per permissible Areas/Activities of IFSCA and thereby identify eligible FinTechs and/or Techfins for their entry into IFSCA's Regulatory/Innovation Sandbox or Authorization.

**Types of Authorization for Accelerators:**

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **I. Limited Use Authorization (LUA)** | For Accelerator desirous to operate from India or any other FATF compliant countries/jurisdictions |
| **II. Authorization** | For Accelerator desirous to operate from GIFT-IFSC (IFSCA may grant 'In principle approval' for a period of **120 days**) |

### 3. What are the eligibility criteria for Accelerators to apply under the FE Framework?

**I. If an Indian Accelerator:**
- An entity registered with DPIIT as start-up related to FinTech; or
- A company or LLP incorporated in India; or
- Branch of Company/LLP in IFSC; or
- An entity working directly or indirectly in the ecosystem regulated by RBI/SEBI/IRDAI/PFRDA

**II. If a Foreign Accelerator:**
- An entity from jurisdictions except those identified as FATF "High-Risk Jurisdictions subject to a Call for Action" (black list)

**III. Additionally, the Accelerator must fulfil a minimum of TWO (02) of the below criteria:**
- Have operated at least one cohort (physical, virtual and/or hybrid) as per Annexure-I of FE Framework
- Have experience in raising funds and/or bringing Investors for supported entities with **minimum cumulative INR Five Crores (₹50,000,000/-)** raised
- Have signed MoU(s) with international Industry or Academic Partners
- Is a recognized Technology Business Incubator (TBI) by DST, Govt of India or received grants from DST, MeitY, BIRAC, NITI Aayog or similar institutions
- Incubated a **minimum of 100 start-ups** in any jurisdiction
- Have incubated a **minimum of 10 FinTech start-ups** in any jurisdiction
- Have been assisted by Central/State Government(s) or any department/agency in India or any FATF compliant jurisdiction

**IV. Additional Mandatory requirement for Authorization:**
- Shall have a **revenue earning track record in at least one of the last three financial years**

### 4. What are the requirements to be fulfilled by an Accelerator subsequent to receiving Authorization/LUA?

**(i) Cohort Specifications:**

Must operate from IFSCs in one of the following forms:
- i. Newly established/incorporated body corporate in the IFSCs; or
- ii. A subsidiary of an Indian or foreign body corporate in IFSCs; or
- iii. A branch of Indian or foreign body corporate in IFSCs
- iv. Shall have a bank account in any of the IFSCs Banking Units to accept remuneration/funds in Specified foreign currencies

### 5. Is an Accelerator eligible for grant?

An Accelerator with Authorization/LUA as a FinTech Entity shall be eligible to apply for grants per focus area per year:

| Location | Number of Grants per Year |
|----------|--------------------------|
| Accelerator **not based in IFSC** | **One grant** |
| Accelerator **based in IFSC** | **Three grants** |

# V. Other Questions

### 1. Can an entity carry out rupee denominated transactions from GIFT City?
### What are the permissible currencies in which entities can transact in GIFT IFSC?

**GIFT City has two Zones:**
- I. Domestic Tariff Area (DTA) - All domestic (Rupee-denominated/INR) transactions can be undertaken
- II. Multiservice Special Economic Zone (SEZ) with International Financial Services Centre

**As per section 20 of IFSCA Act, 2019:**
- Every transaction of financial services in an IFSC shall be in such foreign currency as may be specified by regulations
- All transactions in IFSCs shall be undertaken in **specified foreign currencies** and only via bank transfers
- IFSCA may allow transactions denominated in INR in IFSCs provided such transactions are **settled in the specified foreign currencies**

**Specified Foreign Currencies** (as per First Schedule of IFSCA Banking Regulations, 2020):

### 2. Can an Entity focused on domestic (India) operations only, set up office in GIFT-IFSC?

An entity setting up office in GIFT-IFSC shall have focus on **global outreach and opportunities** and not cater only to domestic Indian market from GIFT IFSC.

**Use cases for setting operations in GIFT IFSC:**
- Landing Pad for Foreign FinTechs - to explore India markets
- GIFT IFSC as a Market
- Launch Pad for Domestic FinTechs - to go global

### 3. When is it mandatory to set up an office in GIFT-IFSC?

| Authorization Type | Office Requirement |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Limited Use Authorization (Sandbox)** | No requirement to set up office at GIFT IFSC during Sandbox period unless a bank account is required to be opened in IFSC |
| **Authorization** | FE shall establish an office in GIFT IFSC and commence operations within **120 days** from Authorization or any such timeframe as may be specified by IFSCA |

### 4. What is the mode of establishing an office/entity in GIFT IFSC?

The Entity shall operate from GIFT-IFSC in either of the following forms:

### 6. What are the relevant capital controls in India?

- GIFT IFSC caters to customers outside the jurisdiction of domestic economy
- GIFT IFSC is a jurisdiction that provides financial services to non-residents and residents (Institutions), in foreign currency other than INR
- IFSC in India is treated as a **"deemed foreign territory"** located on Indian soil with a similar ecosystem at par with other offshore financial service jurisdictions
- As per Foreign Exchange Management (IFSC) Regulations, 2015, any financial institution or branch set up in the IFSC shall be treated as a **person resident outside India**
- **Capital controls applicable in Indian jurisdiction are NOT APPLICABLE in GIFT IFSC**
- However, applicability on profits and dividends may be subject to local tax and company laws

### 7. What are the relevant Withholding Taxes?

The applicability of Withholding Tax pertains to specific activity and is transaction-specific.

**As per Finance Bill, 2023:**
- Beneficial withholding tax rate of **10%** under Section 115A read with Section 80LA for dividends received by non-residents from IFSC units
- Benefit of Section 194LC extended at withholding rate of **9%** to long-term bonds or rupee-denominated bonds issued after July 1, 2023, listed on recognised stock exchanges in IFSC

*A discussion with a Tax Expert is advisable.*

### 8. What is the applicability of Indian Legal System in GIFT IFSC and status of Arbitration mechanism?

- Currently the **Indian Legal System applies** in GIFT-IFSC
- Jurisdiction applicable: **Courts of Gandhinagar District, Gujarat**
- It is also envisaged to set up an **International Arbitration Centre** in GIFT-City for timely settlement of disputes under international jurisprudence
