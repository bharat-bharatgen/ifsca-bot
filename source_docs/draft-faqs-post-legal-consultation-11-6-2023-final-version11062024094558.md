# Part 1: General Information on Finance Company / Finance Unit

### 1. What is a 'Finance Company' (FC)?

**"Finance Company"** refers to a financial institution, as defined under clause (c) of sub-section (1) of section 3 of the IFSCA Act, 2019, **separately incorporated in IFSC** for undertaking one or more permissible activities, provided:

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| (i) | It does **not accept public deposit** from resident and non-resident |
| (ii) | It is **not registered** with the Authority as a Banking Unit |

> **Note:** 'Public deposit' refers to an amount raised from a resident or a non-resident, in any form which is repayable on demand or is a term deposit.

### 2. What is a 'Finance Unit' (FU)?

**'Finance Unit'** refers to a financial institution which is **set up as a branch**, to deal in one or more permissible activities, provided:

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| (i) | It does not accept public deposit from resident and non-resident |
| (ii) | It is not registered with the Authority as a Banking Unit |

**Requirements for FU undertaking core activities:**
| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| (i) | Incorporated entity in its home jurisdiction |
| (ii) | Engaged in the business of financial services |
| (iii) | Regulated by a financial sector regulator in its home jurisdiction |
| (iv) | Obtain **No-Objection Certificate** from the home regulator |

> **Note:** These conditions shall **not apply** to an applicant intending to undertake activity of a **Global/Regional Corporate Treasury Centre** as a Finance Unit.

### 3. What are the legal forms permitted for setting up of a Finance Company?

A Finance Company can be set up as:
- Subsidiary
- Joint venture
- Newly incorporated company under the **Companies Act, 2013**
- Any other form as specified by the Authority

### 4. Is there any requirement on the jurisdiction for promoters/directors/shareholders?

Yes, the promoters/founders/group entities/directors/shareholders/Ultimate beneficial owners (up to a natural person) shall **NOT** be from countries or jurisdictions identified by **FATF as High-Risk Jurisdictions** subject to a Call for Action (commonly referred as **Blacklist**).

The FC/FU shall comply with:
- Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002
- PML rules
- IFSCA (AML/CTF/KYC) Guidelines, 2022

### 5. What activities can a Finance Company/Finance Unit undertake?

| S.No. | Activity |
|-------|----------|
| (i) | **Lend** in the form of loans, commitments and guarantees, credit enhancement, securitisation, financial lease, sale and purchase of portfolios |
| (ii) | **Undertake investments** - subscribing, acquiring, holding, or transferring securities |
| (iii) | **Factoring and Forfaiting** of receivables |
| (iv) | **Buy or Sell derivatives** (as a client) |
| (v) | **Global/Regional Corporate Treasury Centres** |
| (vi) | **Aircraft/Ship Lessor** |
| (vii) | **International Trade Financing Services Platform (ITFS)** |
| (viii) | Any other permitted activity **without customer interface** (including Holding Companies) |

# Part 2: Permissible Activities for FC/FU

### 6. What are the non-core activities that can be undertaken on a standalone basis?

| Activity |
|----------|
| (i) **Operating lease** of any products (aircraft lease, ship lease, or other equipment) |
| (ii) Any permissible activity **without customer interface** (e.g., Holding companies - solely for group entities) |
| (iii) Setting up of **International Trade Financing Services Platform** |
| (iv) Acting as **facilitators** of permissible activities |

### 7. What are the non-core activities that can be undertaken as an additional activity?

These include:
- Investment Advisory Services
- Portfolio Management Services
- Distribution of financial products

> **Explanation:** For carrying out such non-core activities, the FC/FU shall obtain **separate registration** under relevant activity-specific regulations/frameworks.

### 8. Can a Finance Company serve as a Holding Company?

**Yes.** A FC can serve as a holding company under **regulation 5(1)(iii)(m)** of the FC Regulations, which includes engaging in activities **without any customer interface**, providing services only to the **group entities** of the parent.

> **Note:** 'Group Entity' - two or more entities related through: subsidiary-parent (AS 21), Joint venture (AS 27), Associate (AS 23), related party (AS 18), Common brand name, and investment in equity shares (**20% and above**).

### 9. Can a FC/FU undertaking non-core activities also engage in 'investment activities'?

**Yes**, but **only for the purpose of liquidity and balance sheet management** as part of normal business operations. Such 'investment activity' will not be treated as core activity.

### 10. Can a FC/FU undertaking non-core activities carry out derivative transactions?

**Yes**, but only for the purpose of **hedging their underlying exposures** i.e., as a 'client/user'.

### 11. Can an FC/FU undertake multiple core, non-core activities, or both?

**Yes**, subject to applicable activity-specific framework/circulars/regulations, which may include seeking separate license/registration/authorisation.

**Examples:**
- FC with operating lease for aircraft wanting distribution of financial products → separate registration required
- FC with lending wanting aircraft operating lease → separate registration under IFSCA Framework for Aircraft Lease required
- GRCTC wanting to hold equity stake in group entities → separate registration under regulation 5(1)(iii)(m) required

### 12. Does an entity with existing registration need fresh registration under FC Regulations for the same activity?

**No.** An entity that has obtained registration for a specific activity under another IFSCA framework shall **not** be required to seek fresh registration under FC regulations for the same activity.

### 13. Can an already registered entity seek additional registration under FC Regulations?

**Yes**, subject to:
- Obtaining **No-objection Certificate (NoC)** from the Authority under current framework
- Fresh application for activities **solely regulated under FC Regulations**

### 14. Requirements for expanding scope of activities within FC Regulations?

| Requirement |
|-------------|
| (i) MoA should enable the FC/FU to undertake proposed activity |
| (ii) Submit Application letter signed by authorised representative/director |
| (iii) Submit Board approval for expansion |
| (iv) Apply to Administrator (IFSCA) for expanding activities in LoA |
| (v) Submit revised business plan for next **three years** |
| (vi) Pay applicable fees (as per IFSCA Fee Circular dated **May 17, 2023**) |
| (vii) Submit any other document sought by Authority |

# Part 3: Owned Funds for FC/FU

### 15. What is the definition of 'owned fund'?

As per **regulation 2(1)(i)** of the FC Regulations:

**"Owned fund"** = Paid-up capital + Free reserves + Share premium account balance + Capital reserves (from sale proceeds of asset)
**Minus:** Accumulated loss balance + Book value of intangible assets + Deferred revenue expenditure

> Excludes reserves created by revaluation of asset.

### 16. Minimum Owned Fund (MOF) Requirements

| No. | Activity | Minimum Owned Fund (USD or equivalent) |
|-----|----------|----------------------------------------|
| **1** | **Non-core activities only** (without core activities) | Higher of **USD 0.2 million** OR amount required under specific Framework/Regulation; OR any higher amount specified by Authority |
| **2** | **One or more core activities** (with or without non-core, except GRCTC) | Higher of **USD 3 million**, minimum regulatory capital for core activities; OR amount required for non-core under specific Framework; OR any higher amount specified |
| **3** | **Global/Regional Corporate Treasury Centres** | Higher of **USD 0.2 million** OR amount required under relevant regulatory framework |

# Part 4: Prudential and Corporate Governance Requirements

### 17. What are the prudential regulatory requirements for a FC/FU?

**Applicable only for core activities** as per regulation 4 of FC Regulations:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **(i) Capital Ratio (CR)** | Minimum **8%** of regulatory capital to risk weighted assets (IFSCA Circular dated **April 26, 2021**) |
| **(ii) Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)** | Maintain LCR on standalone basis at all times (IFSCA Circular dated **June 24, 2021**). For FU, LCR may be maintained by parent entity with IFSCA approval |
| **(iii) Exposure Ceiling (EC)** | Maximum **25%** of available eligible capital base to single counterparty or group of connected counterparties without Authority approval (IFSCA Circular dated **May 25, 2021**) |

### 18. What are the corporate governance and disclosure requirements?

Specified in IFSCA Circular titled 'Guidelines on Corporate Governance and Disclosure Requirements for a Finance Company' dated **August 09, 2021**.

### 19. Is approval required for change in management of FC/FU?

**Yes**, as per **regulation 8(2)** of FC Regulations:
- Merger, acquisitions, takeovers or change in management resulting in **change in control of at least 20%** of total share capital OR business decisions under agreement → requires **prior approval**

**For FU parent changes:** Shall be intimated to Authority within **15 days** from the date of such event.

### 20. Is there any relaxation from Prudential and CG requirements?

| Entity Type | Relaxation | Conditions |
|-------------|------------|------------|
| **Non-core activities only** | Exempt from regulation 4 (prudential) and regulation 8 (CG) | (i) Board approved prudential policy (ii) Fit and Proper criteria |
| **GRCTC** | Exempt from prudential regulations only | (i) Board approved prudential policy (ii) Fit and Proper criteria |

### 21. Additional legal frameworks for FC/FU undertaking core activities

| Sr. No. | Circular Name | Date |
|---------|---------------|------|
| 1 | Guidelines on distribution of mutual Funds and insurance products by FC/FUs | May 03, 2021 |
| 2 | Information on various guidelines/circulars/directions applicable for non-core activities | May 03, 2021 |
| 3 | Guidance Framework on sustainable and sustainability linked lending by Financial Institutions | April 26, 2022 |

> **Exception:** FC/FU undertaking core activities **solely for group entities** are exempt from prudential and CG guidelines (including exposure norms), except GRCTC which are subject to CG norms.

# Part 5: Application Form

### 22. Where can the application form be found?

**URL:** https://ifsca.gov.in/Pages/Contents/HowToApply

### 23. Preferred mode of submission?

- Digital or physical format
- Preferred: **digitally in readable and searchable format**
- Supporting documents: notarized or apostilled
- Language: **English** with attested translations where applicable

### 24. Email for application submission?

**Primary:** applications@ifsca.gov.in
**Copy to:** financecomp-queries@ifsca.gov.in

### 25. Can a parent with existing FC/FU use simplified application?

**Yes**, can submit 'Simplified Application form for Finance Company' at: https://ifsca.gov.in/Pages/Contents/HowToApply

### 26. Expected time for obtaining registration?

As per IFSCA circular 'Time limit for disposal of Applications' dated **December 26, 2023**:

| Entity Type | Processing Time |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Finance Company (Core) and ITFS** | **90 days** from complete application |
| **Finance Company (Non-Core)** | **60 days** from complete application |
| **General target** | **45 days** |

Authority may also grant **Provisional Registration (PR)** for additional time to comply with conditions.

### 27. Can business commence after provisional registration?

**No.** As per **Regulation 3(1)** of FC Regulations, business shall commence only after obtaining **Certificate of Registration**, subject to conditions therein.

### 28. Expected timeframe for applicant to respond to queries?

- **15 days** from date of communication
- Reminder sent if no response
- If reminder also unanswered for **15 days** → application determined non-compliant
- Entity must file new application and pay fees again

### 29. Is a meeting with Authority necessary during application process?

**No**, but Authority may conduct meeting if found necessary on case-to-case basis.

### 30. Can request for exemption/relaxation be submitted before application?

**No.** Relaxation request shall form part of the application, duly substantiated with rationale and Board concurrence.

# Part 5: Fee Structure

### 31. What are the various kinds of fees applicable?

| Fee Type | Payment Timeline |
|----------|------------------|
| **(i) Application fee** | Along with the application |
| **(ii) Registration fee** | Within **15 days** from grant of provisional registration |
| **(iii) Recurring fee** | Within **30 days** from grant of certificate of registration |

> **Note:** For multiple activities, registration fee and recurring fee apply for **each activity**.

**Reference:** IFSCA Fee Circular No. 865/IFSCA/Banking/Fee Revision/2022-23, dated **May 17, 2023**

### 32. Where shall the fee be remitted?

Bank account number provided in Fee Circular, via **RTGS/NEFT/cheque/Swift payment system**.

### 33. Can payment be made in INR?

- **Application and Registration fee:** Can be remitted in INR
- **All other applicable fees:** USD only

### 34. What reference rate for INR payments?

**RBI reference rate** for the date of remittance: https://www.rbi.org.in/scripts/ReferenceRateArchive.aspx

### 35. For multiple activities, separate payments required?

**Yes**, fees specified for each activity must be paid **separately**.

# Part 6: Information on Management (IOM)

### 36. Who shall submit the 'Information on Management'?

**For FC:** IOM to be filled only by **natural persons** (not body-corporates):
- Promoters
- Founders
- Directors/Partners/Designated Partners
- Key Managerial Personnel
- Persons/Shareholders having **10% and above** share/voting rights/distributable dividend
- Beneficial Owners of a Trust

IOM shall be **self-attested** form submitted separately for each natural person.

**For FU:** Not applicable.

# Part 7: Business Plan

### 37. Key elements in business plan?

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| (i) | Detailed business profile (history, industry presence, market position, target customers) |
| (ii) | Proposed business activity in IFSC with process flows/roadmap |
| (iii) | Business projections for **three consecutive years** (balance sheet, income statement, cash flow with assumptions) |
| (iv) | Proposed source for meeting owned fund requirement |
| (v) | Number of officials proposed to be posted in FC/FU |

# Part 8: Group Structure

### 38. Information required regarding group structure?

- **Vertical structure** depicting promoters with shareholdings up to **ultimate beneficial owner** (natural person)
- Country of incorporation for each entity
- Activities of each entity

**For GRCTC:** Complete group structure and disclose entities it intends to cater at time of application.

# Part 9: Post Registration

### 39. Is Certificate of Registration subject to annual renewal?

**No**, but entity shall:
- Continue to meet **fit and proper** requirements
- Intimate Authority regarding **any material change** in information submitted

> **Note:** If FC/FU fails to fulfil conditions, Authority may take action including suspension, withdrawal or cancellation of registration.

### 40. Post-registration compliance requirements?

| Requirement |
|-------------|
| (i) Adhere to conditions in Certificate of Registration |
| (ii) Adhere to all IFSCA rules, laws, regulations (FEMA, Companies Act, etc.) |
| (iii) Adhere to **IFSCA (AML, CTF and KYC Guidelines), 2022** |
| (iv) Comply with applicable reporting and supervisory requirements |
