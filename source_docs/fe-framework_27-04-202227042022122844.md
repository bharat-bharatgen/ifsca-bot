# Framework for FinTech Entity in the International Financial Services Centres (IFSCs)

**Circular No:** F.No. 521/IFSCA/FinTech/FE Framework/2022-23
**Date:** April 27, 2022
**Subject:** Framework for FinTech Entity in the International Financial Services Centres (IFSCs)
**Issued By:** Joseph Joshy, Chief Technology Officer
**Email for Applications:** fesandbox@ifsca.gov.in
**Total Pages:** 33

## Page 1

## Applicability

(1) This framework shall apply to all eligible **Domestic or Foreign entities**, desirous of obtaining authorization as **FinTech Entity (FE)** from International Financial Services Centres Authority (IFSCA), for participating in or undertaking one or more of the permissible activities under this framework.

## Page 2-3

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Act** | International Financial Services Centres Authority Act, 2019 (50 of 2019) |
| **Applicant** | An entity who makes an application to the Authority for getting authorization as FinTech Entity under this framework |
| **Authority** | International Financial Services Centres Authority |
| **Cohorts or Special Programmes** | Business programmes run by companies or incubators that support early-stage, growth-driven FinTech companies through education, mentorship, etc. |
| **Domestic FinTechs** | An entity that is not a 'Foreign FinTech' |
| **Domestic Financial Sector Regulator** | RBI, SEBI, IRDAI, and PFRDA |
| **Evaluation Committee** | Internal committee constituted by the Authority for screening, evaluation and processing of applications |
| **FinTech** | Financial technology solutions which result in new business models, applications, process or products in financial services regulated by the Authority OR advanced/innovative technology solutions which aid and assist activities in relation to financial products, financial services and financial institutions |
| **FinTech Entity** | An entity authorized by the Authority under the appropriate framework (includes both Domestic and Foreign FinTechs) |
| **Foreign FinTechs** | A non-resident entity from outside India engaged in the FinTech activity |
| **Innovation Sandbox** | Testing environment where FinTech Entities can test their ideas and solutions in **isolation from the live market** based on market related data |
| **Inter-Operable Regulatory Sandbox (IORS)** | Testing environment for innovative hybrid financial products/services falling within the regulatory ambit of more than one domestic financial sector regulator |
| **Regulatory Sandbox** | **Live environment with a limited set of real customers** for a limited timeframe wherein entities shall be granted certain exemptions/relaxations |
| **Overseas Regulatory Referral** | Co-operation mechanism between the Authority and any overseas financial sector regulator |

## Page 3-5

# Chapter I: Authorization

## Eligibility

(4) An Applicant who satisfies any of the following conditions shall be eligible:

### Where the Applicant is from India:

| Condition | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| (a) | Entity registered with **DPIIT** as a start-up entity relating to FinTech |
| (b) | Entity incorporated as a **company under Companies Act 2013**, or as **LLP under LLP Act, 2008** or a **Branch** of an Indian company or LLP in IFSC |
| (c) | Entity working directly or indirectly in the ecosystem regulated by domestic financial sector regulator |

### Where the Applicant is from Outside India:

- An entity from **FATF compliant countries/jurisdictions**

(5) The Applicant proposes to use **technology in its core product or service**, business model, distribution model or methodology to solve the problem being targeted.

(6) The Applicant must have:
- (a) A **deployable solution/working product**; and
- (b) A **revenue earning track record** in at least one of the last three financial years

## Permissible Activities

(7) An Applicant shall be permitted to undertake **one** of the following activities:

| Activity Type | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| **(a) FinTech** | Provide a financial technology solution which results in new business models, applications, process, or products in financial services regulated by the Authority |
| **(b) TechFin** | Provide an advanced or emerging technology solution in allied areas/activities which aid and assist activities in relation to financial products, financial services and financial institutions |

## Application Process and Evaluation

| Clause | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| (8) | Apply by way of application at **Annexure-II** |
| (9) | Application signed by Authorized person |
| (10) | Submit at **fesandbox@ifsca.gov.in** |
| (11) | Application subject to evaluation by Evaluation Committee |

## Authorization as FinTech Entity (FE)

(12) The Authority may grant Authorization as 'FinTech Entity' subject to:
- (a) Separately incorporating an entity in the IFSC; or
- (b) Establishing a branch or subsidiary of an Indian or foreign incorporated entity in IFSC

(13) If Authorization cannot be granted, Authority shall communicate deficiencies giving **thirty days' time** to rectify.

## Maintenance of Books of Accounts, Records and Documents

(15) Every FinTech Entity shall maintain its records, documents and books of accounts in such **freely convertible foreign currency other than INR**, as declared at the time of application.

## Submissions of Report/Information

| Clause | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| (16)(a) | Duly certified copy of audited annual financial statements within **30 days** of finalization |
| (16)(b) | Details of regulatory action within **15 days** from date of receipt of notice |

(23) The FinTech Entity shall transact in **freely convertible foreign currency only**. However, may defray administrative expenses in INR by maintaining an **SNRR account**.

# Chapter II: Sandbox

## Permissible Activities

(24) An Applicant shall be permitted to undertake:
- (a) Test FinTech ideas in **IFSCA FinTech Regulatory Sandbox**
- (b) Develop and test ideas in **IFSCA FinTech Innovation Sandbox**
- (c) Test ideas in **Inter-Operable Regulatory Sandbox (IoRS)**
- (d) Provide ideas via **Overseas Regulatory Referral mechanism/FinTech Bridge**

## Part A: IFSCA FinTech Regulatory Sandbox (FRS)

### Eligibility

Same as Chapter I eligibility criteria.

(26) The Applicant proposes to use **innovative technology** in its core product or service to provide financial services that are or likely to be regulated by the Authority.

### Eligibility Criteria for the Proposed Solution

| Criteria | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **(a) Genuineness of Innovation** | Solution should be innovative enough to add significant value to existing offering |
| **(b) Genuine Need to Test** | Genuine need for live testing on real customers/investors |
| **(c) Limited Prior Testing** | Limited offline testing should have been carried out before applying |
| **(d) Direct Benefits to Users** | Solution should offer identifiable benefits (direct or indirect) |
| **(e) No Risks to Financial System** | Proper risk management strategy with appropriate safeguards |
| **(f) Testing Readiness** | Necessary resources to support testing with well-developed testing plans |
| **(g) Deployment Post-Testing** | Demonstrate intention and ability to deploy on broader scale |

### Application Process

| Clause | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| (28) | Apply via **Annexure-III** |
| (31) | Applicant informed of potential suitability within **30 working days** of complete application |
| (33) | **30 days** time to rectify deficiencies |

### Approval Process within Sandbox

(36) IFSCA may accord **Limited Use Authorization** as "FinTech Entity (FE)" to develop and test in the sandbox.

(39) **Duration of sandbox testing: Maximum 12 months** which may be extended further by **6 months** upon request.

### Evaluation Criteria within Sandbox

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| (i) | Profile of the FE |
| (ii) | Usage of innovative solution including technology and/or processes |
| (iii) | Identified benefits to customers/investors |
| (iv) | Compilation of meaningful test scenarios |
| (v) | Risk measured/graded testing conditions |
| (vi) | Appropriate disclosure requirements |
| (vii) | Clearly defined grievance redressal mechanism |
| (viii) | Adequate disclosure of potential risks to users |
| (ix) | Prior confirmation from users on accepting risks |
| (x) | Intent and feasibility to deploy post testing |
| (xi) | Deployment/monitoring strategy or exit strategy |
| (xii) | Any other factors considered relevant |

### Regulatory Exemptions

(42) Authority may consider granting exemptions/relaxations. However, **NO exemptions** from:
- Customer/investor protection
- Know-Your-Customer (KYC) requirements
- Anti-Money Laundering (AML) rules

### Submission of Test Related Information

(46) FE shall submit **final report within 30 days** from expiry of testing period containing:
- Key outcomes and KPIs
- Full account of incident reports and resolution of complaints
- Key learnings from the test

(48) FE shall maintain test records for **5 years** from completion of testing/exit.

### Extending or Exiting the Sandbox

(51) At end of testing period, Limited Use Authorization shall **expire**.

(52) Upon completion:
- (a) Authority shall decide whether to permit wider scale introduction
- (b) FE may carry out regulated business under IFSCA licensing OR modified regulatory dispensation
- (c) If not allowed wider scale: employ exit strategy OR request extension

(53) FE may exit on its own by giving **30 working days** prior written notice.

(55) FE shall maintain records of user acknowledgements for **5 years** from exit.

### Revocation of Approval

(56) IFSCA may revoke approval if FE:
- Fails to put in place risk mitigating measures
- Submits false/misleading information
- Contravenes any law
- Suffers loss of reputation
- Insolvency/liquidation order passed
- Compromises digital security
- Carries on business detrimental to users
- Fails to address technical defects
- Fails to implement Authority directions

(59) Upon revocation, FE must:
- Immediately implement exit plan
- Notify users about cessation
- Comply with obligations to dispose confidential information
- Submit report within **30 days** from revocation

---

## Part B: IFSCA FinTech Innovation Sandbox (FIS)

(62) **Innovation Sandbox** - Testing environment where FinTech firms can test solutions in **isolation from the live market**, based on market related data made available by regulated entities in IFSC.

(65) Authority may grant **'Limited Use Authorization'** as 'FinTech Entity' to develop and test in FIS.

(67) Upon successful exit from FIS, applicant shall be eligible to apply for **IFSCA FinTech Regulatory Sandbox**.

(68) FE may exit by giving **30 working days** prior notice.

---

## Part C: Inter-operable Regulatory Sandbox (IoRS) with IFSCA as Principal Regulator

(72) Foreign FinTechs seeking entry to India shall be considered for IoRS provided:
- Entity must have a financial technology product/service whose business models fall within the remit of **more than one domestic financial sector regulator**

(75) Post successful exit from IoRS, entity shall approach Authority and Associate Regulator(s) for authorization before launching.

---

## Part D: Overseas Regulatory Referral Mechanism/FinTech Bridges of IFSCA

(77) Governed as per provisions of **MoU or collaboration** between the Authority and corresponding overseas Financial Sector Regulator(s).

## Page 29-31: Annexure-IV, V, VI (Process Flowcharts)

# Annexure-IV: Direct Authorization Process Overview

**Process Flow:**
1. Applicant submits application to IFSCA
2. Pre-Screening: checks eligibility, relevance, priority focus areas (by FD and BV)
3. KYC, AML, Eligibility Criteria (by FD & Legal)
4. Initial Screening - Pitch by FinTech (by FD and BV)
5. FD Prepares "Background Note" for Evaluation Committee
6. Evaluation Committee recommends to Competent Authority (FinTech makes a Pitch)
7. **Authorization as FinTech Entity (FE)** to operate in GIFT IFSC

---

# Annexure-V: IFSCA FinTech Regulatory Sandbox (FRS) Process Overview

**Process Flow:**
1. **Application and Screening Process** - IFSCA informs potential suitability to sandbox
2. **Evaluation Stage** - Option to apply for IFSCA FinTech Incentive Scheme 2022
3. Approval process within Sandbox
4. **Limited Use Authorization as FinTech Entity (FE)**
5. **Testing Stage** - FinTech Regulatory Sandbox
6. Testing Phase → Successful Exit → **Authorization as FE to operate in GIFT IFSC** under appropriate Regulatory Framework

---

# Annexure-VI: IFSCA FinTech Innovation Sandbox (FIS) Process Overview

**Process Flow:**
1. **Application and Screening Process** - IFSCA informs potential suitability to sandbox
2. **Evaluation Stage** - Option to apply for IFSCA FinTech Incentive Scheme 2022
3. Approval process within Sandbox
4. **Limited Use Authorization as FinTech Entity (FE)**
5. **Testing Stage** - FinTech Innovation Sandbox
6. Testing Phase → Successful Exit → **Apply for IFSCA FinTech Regulatory (FRS) Sandbox**

# Annexure-VII: Requirements - Relaxation Policy

## Requirements for which relaxation CANNOT be considered:

| Requirement |
|-------------|
| Confidentiality of customer information |
| Fit and proper criteria particularly on honesty and integrity |
| Handling of customer's moneys and assets by intermediaries |
| Prevention of money laundering and countering the financing of terrorism |
| Risk checks (price check, order value check, etc.) |
| Principles of KYC |

## Requirements that MAY merit relaxation:

| Requirement |
|-------------|
| Net worth |
| Track record |
| Registration fees |
| IFSCA Guidelines (technology risk management, outsourcing) |
| Financial soundness |
| Board composition |
| Management experience |
| Asset maintenance requirement |
